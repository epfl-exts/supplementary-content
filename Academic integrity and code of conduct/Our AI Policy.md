### Academic integrity and code of conduct

As a participant in this course, it is your responsibility to ensure that all work submitted, including source code, analysis, and documentation, is the result of your own intellectual effort.
The use of Generative AI tools (such as ChatGPT, Copilot, etc.) must be treated as using an external source, and failing to follow the **declaration instruction** provided by the instructors constitutes a  disciplinary offence under EPFL's directives. We invite you to review Art. 2 of [EPFL Ordinance on disciplinary measures](https://www.epfl.ch/about/overview/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/2.4.0.2Disciplinary_Rules_Regulations_ang.pdf) on academic disciplinary offences: 

*An academic disciplinary offence is considered to have been committed by a person if they behave in a fraudulent manner within the context of projects completed as part of their studies or during an assessment, if they attempt to behave in a fraudulent manner, or [...]:*
 
- *a. by gaining access to or using means which are not expressly authorised;* 
- *b. by violating the general regulations or specific instructions on examinations;*
- *c. by presenting work in which they claim for their own, wholly or partially, work which has been completed by a third party (plagiarism) or [...].*

### Why this matters

Based on our experience, learners who rely heavily on external help (e.g., AI tools) without building sufficient understanding of the context often struggle to:

- Pass both parts of the assessment due to insufficient knowledge of concepts, methods and workflows.
- Fully understand the assisted solution.
- Verify the correctness of the solution or detect potential errors.
- Extend or modify the solution when needed.
- Develop critical thinking around data.
  
By engaging directly with the challenges of this course, you ensure that the skills you acquire are robust, transferable, and ethically sound. Additionally, ensuring that all learners are evaluated on an even playing field helps maintain fairness and promotes an equitable learning experience for everyone.
