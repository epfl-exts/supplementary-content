## Assessment framework
### Assessment process

The assessment process begins once you submit your project and ends once you receive the final grade. **Your subscription must be active during this process**.

The assessment has two exams:

1. **A written exam**: a Jupyter Notebook(s) submission 
   
2. **An oral exam**: a 30 minutes online examination to assess the competencies and skills acquired throughout the course

The assessment will happen in the following ways:

1. Within two weeks after the submission date of your project, a course instructor will contact you to coordinate a time for the oral exam. 
2. You will receive the necessary instructions for the oral exam which will happen in an online session.
3. Within one week after the oral examination date, you will receive the final grade and an overall feedback to your performance.
4. In case of a Fail decision, the feedback will include the issues in the area(s) that can be improved. In this case, it is possible to repeat both assessments once.

### Final grade
1. **Grading scale**: The final performance is assessed on a Pass/Fail basis.
2. **Criteria for success**: To obtain the Microcredential, you must demonstrate proficiency across all assessment areas. The requirements are structured as follows:
   
    **a) Final grade**: A Pass is awarded only if you receive a Pass grade in **both** the written and the oral examinations.

    **b) Written and oral examinations**: To receive a Pass for either the written or oral examination, you must achieve a Pass in **all** specific assessment areas defined within that component.

    **c) Assessment areas**: Within each assessment area, the threshold for a Pass is set at 80%. You must provide correct responses or demonstrate the required competence for at least 80% of the evaluated items or criteria.

### Assessment areas and their description
**Written exam**

1. **Formatting and layout of the Notebook**: The submitted project must adhere to the specific layout and format requirements instructed in the course
2. **Analysis and communication of decisions and results**: Be able to communicate the results and provide insights from the results (English fluency, grammar, and spelling are not part of the assessment criteria)
3. **Coding proficiency**: Be able to efficiently use Python libraries taught in the course

**Oral exam**

1. **Subject knowledge**: Be able to demonstrate subject knowledge at reasonable depth
2. **Analysis and communication of decisions and results**: Be able to answer questions that arise from the discussions and explanations provided by the learner in their project
3. **Coding proficiency**:
    - Be able to answer questions that arise from the code snippets provided by the learner in their project
    - Be able to write codes that are relevant to the scope of the project
